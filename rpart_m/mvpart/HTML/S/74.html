  50 /**************************************************************************
  51 **
  52 **  Split:
  53 **      variable number of the split; 0 = no more surrogates (or primaries)
  54 **
  55 **      split point: the actual split point for a continuous
  56 **
  57 **      improve:  For primary splits, the iprovement index returned by the
  58 **                 bsplit routine.  This is the measure that determines the
  59 **                 winning split.
  60 **                For surrogate splits, this holds the error rate, i.e., the
  61 **                 % incorrect guesses of the primary by using this surrogate.
  62 **
  63 **      count: The number of observations split using this variable.  For the
  64 **             first primary, this will = the number of non-missing values.
  65 **             For surrogates, it will be the number missing in the primary
  66 **             and all earlier surrogates but not missing on this one.  (For
  67 **             all primaries but the first, the number is theoretical).
  68 **
  69 **  adj:  Let "maj" be the %agreement for going with the majority,
  70 **                and "agree" the %agreement for this surrogate.  The
  71 **                adjusted value is (agree - maj)/(1-maj); the amount of
  72 **                the potential improvement actually realized.  The denominator
  73 **                for both percents depends on the sur_agree option.
  74 **
  75 **      csplit[0]:   For a continuous variable, we also need to know the
  76 **                    direction of the split.  We use this "extra" variable
  77 **                    as 1: <x to the left, -1: <x to the right.
  78 **
  79 **      csplit[]:    For a categorical, the labels are LEFT, RIGHT, and
  80 **                    0=missing.  (Even if a particular category is not empty,
  81 **                    there may be no subjects from that category present
  82 **                    at a particular split further down the tree).
  83 **
  84 **
  85 **  Node:
  86 **      num_obs: Number of observations in the node.
  87 **
  88 **      response_est: From the eval routine.  Estimate of the response, if
  89 **                      this node were terminal.
  90 **
  91 **      risk: From the eval routine. Estimate of risk, if this node were
  92 **                      terminal.
  93 **
  94 **      complexity: On the way down, it holds equation 5.18.  On the way up
  95 **              it holds a provisional C.P. (The actual C.P. for each node
  96 **              will be the minimum of this number and the provisional C.P.
  97 **              of all nodes above it.  One more pass downward can establish
  98 **              the proper C.P.).
  99 **
 100 **      lastsurrogate: Which direction to send obs for which the primary and
 101 **              all the surrogates are missing.  (The child with the greatest
 102 **      sum of weights).
 103 */
